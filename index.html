1. Introduction & Problem Statement

Current Chatbot Summary

The existing HypertenCare platform is a comprehensive digital health intervention designed for South African communities, focusing on hypertension management through accessible health education. The platform is tailored to the unique South African context, where 6.8 million people live with hypertension, and 46% are unaware of their condition. Its key strengths include:

  • Accessibility on any mobile phone and support for all 11 official South African languages.
  • Interactive tools like a 15-question risk assessment, educational games (blood pressure simulator, lifestyle choice game), and memory matching activities.
  • Direct links to over 3,500 public clinics, emergency contacts, and community health worker support, aligning with NHI principles.
  • Cultural sensitivity and affordability, making it a vital resource within the public healthcare system.

The current chatbot, built on the WotNot platform, acts as a "black box" with limited transparency, control, and contextual understanding. This presents a critical limitation for a health-focused application, where trust and accuracy are paramount.

Identified Limitation & Opportunity

AI Solution: Plan, Do, Check, Adjust Framework

The proposed solution leverages Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to combine the conversational power of Large Language Models (LLMs) with the accuracy of a curated knowledge base. The implementation uses:

  • NotebookLM for knowledge curation and synthesis.
  • Google Gemini for semantic understanding and response generation.
  • Pinecone for vectorized knowledge storage and retrieval.
  • n8n for workflow automation and backend orchestration.
  • Figma for a responsive, accessible frontend.

The Plan-Do-Check-Adjust (PDCA) framework guided the development:

  • Plan: Design an AI-enhanced architecture integrating RAG with HypertenCareâ€™s existing features.
  • Do: Implement the system using n8n, NotebookLM, and Google Gemini, preserving the platformâ€™s UX and accessibility.
  • Check: Validate performance with South African user scenarios, focusing on cultural sensitivity and integration with clinic directories.
  • Adjust: Refine the system based on user feedback and testing.
